# Sprint-Review-and-Retrospective
How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating "user stories" help with this?
- After gathering information on the user expections from a product, I am able to understand what the user wants and the expected outcomes. Using this information, I am able to determine what functinonality and features should be built to meet the users expectations. I implement these features into techincal tasks to build a product that aligns with the users goals and vision. Creating user stories help me to keep the focus on the user by understanding who the feature is for and why it matters. User stories also foster collabooration amongst the team, prioritize high value features and break large features into smaller manageable tasks.

How do I approach developing programs? What agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
- When approaching program development, I focus on understanding the program and expectations first then I focus on building solutions in small, manageable steps. One Agile process I hope to incorporate in my future development work is the creation of user stories. User stories help to translate the user needs into clear development goals by describing what the user wants and why it is important. This will help me to build high quality products that meets the expectations of the users.

What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
- Being a good team member in software development means working together effectively with other people to solve problems and deliver values. A good team member communicates claerly with the rest of the team, collaborates with other team members, realiable, takes accountability and maintain transparency amongst the team to help the rest of the team deliver a high quality successful product.
